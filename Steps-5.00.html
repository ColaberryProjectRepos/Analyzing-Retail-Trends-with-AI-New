Case 2: Maximizing High-Value Customer Retention
Objective:
To focus retention efforts on high-value customers, specifically those with the highest Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), to enhance revenue and customer loyalty.
Implementation:
The company identified Brenda Dunn as the customer with the highest CLV, totaling $4,818.07. To ensure continued engagement, the company implemented a VIP loyalty program, offering exclusive discounts, early access to new products, and personalized service tailored to high-value customers like Brenda.


Positive Impact:
Enhanced Customer Loyalty: The VIP program strengthened the bond with high-value customers, leading to a 12% increase in CLV across the top customer segment. This not only increased immediate revenue but also contributed to long-term customer loyalty and repeat purchases. 

Negative Impact:
Resource Allocation: Focusing heavily on high-value customers like Brenda Dunn required significant resources. This shift led to a reduction in attention and marketing efforts for lower-tier customers, resulting in a 5% decline in engagement and sales among this group during the same period.
